Intro

Inspiration

We were greatly inspired by the extraordinary utility of Ebay and wanted to capitalize on their interesting Api to improve user experience during exploration of products on the website. Ebay is such a large marketplace of all varieties of unique items as well as consumer products. From basic necessities to collector's items, Ebay provides an amazing way for those across the world to buy and sell their own personal items with others. The ability to connect the world is such a way is immensely empowering. Our chrome extension delves into this field and uses the capabilities of Ebay to provide a great service to users throughout the web shopping process.

What it does

For the vast majority of users buying products on Ebay, they bid on products without really caring which product they end up getting. They are trying to buy products for a better deal and at a lower than the market price for that product. Each of these users have a bidding cap that they really don't want to go over for the item they want. If someone outbids them, they have to manually go and search for a new item to bid on. For example, a user could want an iPhone on Ebay and have a maximum budget of $x. If they are outbidded, then they have to go search for another product and bid again. If there were Ebay bidding queues, then this would not be a problem. Users would be able to set up queues through a Chrome extension. Our server automagically bids a maximum amount for the user on each of the items in the queue. If you are outbid, the server will remove that item from the queue and move onto the next.
